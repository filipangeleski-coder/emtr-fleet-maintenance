Optional add-ons

Stop losing jobs to missed calls and slow replies.

A short rundown of the customer-capture tools we can bolt onto the EMTR site and phone. Every one of these catches work you're currently losing when you're under a truck or asleep at 2am. Real costs, what each does, and how it plugs in.

The problem worth fixing first. A 24/7 mobile mechanic misses calls constantly, on a job, driving, or off the tools. A caller whose truck is down won't leave a voicemail; they ring the next bloke. Industry data puts missed-call recovery at 30-60% of those leads if you text them back within a minute. That's the single highest-return automation for a business like this, and it doesn't even live on the website. It lives with the phone.

The tools

Listed in the order I'd switch them on. The website's enquiry form is already built and free; these add the automation around it.

Missed-call text-back

Start here

Any call you don't pick up triggers an instant SMS within ~30 seconds: "On a job right now, send your vehicle, the fault and your location and I'll call you straight back." Keeps the lead warm instead of letting them ring a competitor. Highest ROI of anything here.

Running cost

~AU$15-45/mo (DIY)

Twilio number US$8.25/mo + ~US$0.05 per SMS sent. Or a managed tool from ~US$15-65/mo.

How it plugs in

Sits on a dedicated business mobile number that forwards to your phone. Independent of the website, works even before the site is live.

Website AI enquiry assistant

Start here

A chat box on the site that talks a visitor through the details that actually matter, vehicle and make, the fault, how urgent (breakdown vs service), and location, then fires a clean, structured lead straight to your phone and email. No more vague "call me" messages. For non-urgent work it can take a soft "what day suits" without locking a rigid calendar slot.

Running cost

~AU$10-25/mo (DIY)

Runs on Claude Haiku, under 1 cent per conversation, plus small hosting. Off-the-shelf (Tidio + AI) runs ~US$68-150/mo.

How it plugs in

A single line of code on the site. No new system to learn; leads land in your inbox/SMS.

After-hours auto-reply

Included

Outside set hours, anyone who calls or messages gets an instant reply that you run 24/7 for genuine breakdowns and will confirm scheduled work in the morning. Sets expectations, stops the "did they even get my message" worry, and flags true emergencies.

Running cost

~AU$0 extra

A time-of-day rule on the two tools above, not a separate subscription.

How it plugs in

Configured once. Switches itself on and off by the clock.

Automatic review requests

Phase 2

After a job's done, an automatic SMS asks the customer for a Google review with a one-tap link. Google reviews are the number-one driver of both local trust and ranking, and right now that's the biggest gap. Worth doing once the lead-capture above is live, no point amplifying reputation before you've stopped leaking leads.

Running cost

cents per review (DIY)

One SMS per finished job. Managed (NiceJob) is US$75/mo if you'd rather it run itself.

How it plugs in

Triggered when you mark a job done. Uses the same SMS number as tool 1.
